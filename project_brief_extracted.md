__Description and overview: __Impact Cornwall CIC identifies and addresses the real areas of need throughout Cornwall and the Isles of Scilly\. Our aspiration is to become the central source for all Cornish stats by providing data to organisations like Cornwall Community Foundation that produce comprehensive Vital Issues and Vital Signs reports that support decision\-making and collaboration among those working to improve local community outcomes\.

# PROJECT DETAILS

__Project area: __Research and Analysis / Website and Project tasks

__Project summary: __The student will work with the Vital Stats Catalogue, a database covering important socio\-economic factors such as deprivation, fuel poverty, and education across Cornwall\.

__Project tasks:__

- __Task 1: __Research and locate missing data sources\. Many data points in the catalogue are currently listed as "Not available\." You'll identify and find the original source URLs for these statistics, such as population figures from the ONS or employment rates from Nomis\.
- __Task 2: __Explore automation opportunities\. For students with technical skills or an interest in learning, there's an opportunity to design or prototype an automated process to retrieve updated data from these sources\. This could range from simple documentation of how data could be updated to building a working solution, depending on your experience level\.
- __Task 3: __Validate key statistics\. You'll check and verify important data points \(such as changes in Universal Credit claimants or disability rates\) to ensure the catalogue remains accurate and trustworthy\.

__Support provided: __We will provide an initial briefing to outline the project goals and desired outputs, with regular check\-ins every two to four weeks to support your progress\.

__Project scope: __Think of this as being the architect of a "live" library\. Currently, the CIC has a library full of valuable books \(data points\), but the information in them is static and some of the original "author" links are missing\. Your job is to track down those missing sources and find ways to keep the library updated, ensuring it remains the most trusted data resource in the county\. For students with technical interests, there's an additional opportunity to design a system that could automatically update the library whenever new information becomes available\.
